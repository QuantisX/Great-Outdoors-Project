Overview of Hiking in Washington

Washington State is known for its diverse landscapes, from lush forests and alpine meadows to rugged coastline and towering peaks. With over 3,000 miles of hiking trails, Washington offers something for every level of hiker.

Top Hiking Trails in Washington

Mount Rainier National Park

Famous for its towering peak, Mount Rainier offers stunning views and challenging trails for all levels. The Skyline Trail is one of the most popular hikes in the park, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

North Cascades National Park

The North Cascades are known for their rugged beauty, with pristine lakes, jagged peaks, and alpine meadows. The Cascade Pass Trail is a must-do for those looking to experience the heart of the Cascades.

Olympic National Park

Olympic National Park is home to a wide variety of ecosystems, from temperate rainforests to coastal cliffs. The Hurricane Ridge Trail offers panoramic views of the Olympic Mountains and the Puget Sound.

Columbia River Gorge

Located on the border between Washington and Oregon, the Columbia River Gorge offers dramatic views of waterfalls, cliffs, and rivers. The Eagle Creek Trail is one of the most scenic hikes in the area.

Hiking Tips for Washington Trails

  • Prepare for the weather: Washington weather can change quickly, especially in the mountains. Be prepared for rain, wind, and sudden temperature drops.
  • Bring enough water and food: Stay hydrated and bring snacks to keep your energy up during long hikes.
  • Wear proper footwear: Hiking boots with good traction are essential for tackling the rocky and uneven terrain in Washington.
  • Respect the environment: Leave no trace by packing out all your trash and staying on designated trails.
